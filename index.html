01 · Executive Summary

Rooftop solar across seven sites. One district. Real impact.

The Rotary Club of Lake District Moinabad and Premier Energies have deployed 12,361 kW of rooftop solar across seven sites in Telangana, from an orphanage in Hyderabad to 355 homes in the village of Kondareddypalli.

The logic behind the project is uncomplicated: when a community organization with deep local trust partners with an industrial manufacturer that builds panels for a living, solar stops being a policy aspiration and becomes something people can actually use.

The two Premier Energies manufacturing facilities (PEPPL and PEGEPL) alone generate over 14.3 million kWh of clean energy annually, avoiding 11,781 tonnes of CO₂ and saving ₹10.75 crores in energy costs each year.

But the story worth telling is smaller in scale: children at Shishu Vihar studying after dusk because the lights stay on, 355 families in Kondareddypalli planning their days around power they actually have, a crematorium in Hyderabad running cleanly and without interruption.

It's not just electricity. It's the fact that we don't have to think about it anymore.

Caregiver, Shishu Vihar Orphanage
02 · Background

Why this matters in Telangana

Much of rural Telangana still contends with unreliable grid power. Households and institutions fill the gaps with diesel generators and kerosene, which are expensive to run, damaging to breathe, and entirely avoidable in a state with 300+ days of usable sunlight per year.

Premier Energies, one of India's largest integrated solar manufacturers, saw a clear fit between its CSR mandate and Rotary's ground-level reach. The Rotary Club of Lake District Moinabad, chartered in 2021 (Club ID 222166, R.I. District 3150), brought the harder-to-build asset: trust. Members knew which sites would benefit most and could coordinate access where outsiders couldn't.

Site selection was deliberate. The team chose locations based on need, not visibility: orphanages, crematoriums, community halls, and residential clusters passed over by conventional grid expansion.

The initiative's launch at Shishu Vihar was led by Smt. A. Nirmala Kanthi Wesley, IAS, Director, Women Child Development & Scheduled Caste (WCD&SC), Government of Telangana, lending state-level endorsement and opening the door for future government collaboration.

We didn't pick locations for optics. We picked them because they'd been waiting the longest.

Rtn. Chiranjeev Singh Saluja, Past President, RCLD Moinabad & MD, Premier Energies

08 · Sustainability Vision

Built to last. Designed to spread.

Environmental

11,781+ tonnes of CO₂ avoided annually across corporate sites. Each community rooftop prevents up to 20 tonnes per year, the equivalent of planting 1,000 trees per site, every year.

Zero noise. Zero emissions. The panels run silently and indefinitely, fitting into village life rather than disrupting it.

Economic

₹10.75 crores (over $1.2 million) in annual savings at PEPPL and PEGEPL, directly strengthening corporate financial resilience. Reduced energy bills at Shishu Vihar and community sites free institutional budgets for core services.

In Kondareddypalli, lower household energy costs mean more money stays in the local economy.

Social & Institutional

Government-attended inaugurations create public accountability. GPS and photo-verified documentation enables audit-ready reporting for future grants.

The model is replicable by design: aligned incentives, clear roles, shared values. Other Rotary clubs and CSR-active companies can adopt the same structure in their own districts.

This isn't a donation.
It's infrastructure.

Resilience built into rooftops. Dignity powered by sunlight. A model that works because it was designed around need, and built to be copied.
